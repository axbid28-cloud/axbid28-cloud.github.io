Each roadmap is carefully structured with phases, checklists, key skills, certifications, and curated resources to guide your transition. Select your desired career path below to explore the detailed roadmap tailored for your goal.

Roadmap: Technical Support Engineer → Project Manager

Overview: Project Managers orchestrate the planning, execution, and delivery of individual projects. This transition leverages your technical troubleshooting skills, customer empathy, and cross-functional collaboration experience while building capabilities in scope management, stakeholder coordination, and strategic planning.

Aspect Technical Support Engineer Project Manager
Primary Focus Resolving customer technical issues Delivering projects on time, within budget, meeting scope
Time Horizon Immediate (ticket-by-ticket) Short to medium-term (project lifecycle)
Key Stakeholders Customers, engineering teams Executives, cross-functional teams, clients, vendors
Success Metrics Resolution time, customer satisfaction, SLA compliance On-time delivery, budget adherence, scope completion, stakeholder satisfaction
Decision Authority Technical solutions within scope Resource allocation, timeline adjustments, risk mitigation

Phase 1: Foundation Building (Months 1-6)

Duration: 6 months | Effort: 10-15 hours/week

Goal: Develop foundational PM knowledge and begin applying concepts

Pro Tip: Start by shadowing existing project managers in your organization. Observe their planning meetings, status updates, and stakeholder interactions to understand the rhythm of project work.

Core Competencies to Build

Project Planning

Work breakdown structures, Gantt charts, critical path analysis, resource allocation

Stakeholder Management

Identification, analysis, communication planning, expectation management

Risk Management

Risk identification, assessment, mitigation planning, contingency strategies

Communication

Status reporting, presentations, meeting facilitation, conflict resolution

Action Checklist

  • Complete Google Project Management Professional Certificate (Coursera) or equivalent foundational PM course
  • Read "A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK® Guide)" - foundational chapters
  • Shadow 2-3 project managers in your organization; attend their planning and review meetings
  • Volunteer to coordinate a small internal team project (e.g., knowledge base update, process improvement)
  • Learn a PM tool: Microsoft Project, Jira, Asana, or Monday.com - complete introductory tutorials
  • Create a work breakdown structure (WBS) for a hypothetical support improvement project
  • Join PMI (Project Management Institute) as a member; access free resources and webinars
  • Identify 2-3 mentors: current PMs willing to provide guidance and answer questions

Recommended Resources

  • Google Project Management Professional Certificate - Coursera
  • PMBOK® Guide - Project Management Institute
  • Project Management Courses - edX
  • ProjectManagement.com - Articles and Community
  • Asana Project Management 101 Guide

Phase 2: Practical Application & Skill Development (Months 7-12)

Duration: 6 months | Effort: 15-20 hours/week

Goal: Lead small projects and build demonstrable PM experience

Advanced Skills Development

Budget Management

Cost estimation, budget tracking, variance analysis, financial reporting

Agile Methodology

Scrum framework, sprint planning, retrospectives, backlog management

Change Management

Change request process, impact assessment, stakeholder buy-in

Quality Management

Quality standards, testing strategies, acceptance criteria, deliverable reviews

Action Checklist

  • Obtain Certified Associate in Project Management (CAPM®) certification from PMI
  • Lead 2-3 small cross-functional projects (e.g., system migration, tool implementation, process redesign)
  • Complete Agile/Scrum training and earn Scrum Master certification (CSM or PSM I)
  • Create detailed project plans with timelines, resources, budgets, and risk registers for each project
  • Conduct weekly status meetings and produce formal status reports for stakeholders
  • Build a project portfolio documenting your projects, outcomes, and lessons learned
  • Participate in PMI local chapter meetings and networking events (online or in-person)
  • Master advanced PM tool features: dashboards, reporting, resource management, dependencies
  • Study for PMP® certification: complete 35 contact hours and document 36 months project experience

Key Milestones

Successfully deliver 2 projects on time and within budget
Earn CAPM® or equivalent entry-level PM certification
Receive positive stakeholder feedback on project leadership
Build portfolio with 3+ documented projects

Recommended Resources

  • Certified ScrumMaster (CSM) - Scrum Alliance
  • Project Management Courses - Udemy
  • Project Management Learning Paths - LinkedIn Learning
  • Agile Project Management Guide - Atlassian

Leadership & Strategic Skills

Action Checklist

  • Lead at least one medium-complexity project ($50K+ budget or 6+ month duration)
  • Request formal PM responsibilities: update job title internally or take Project Coordinator role
  • Develop expertise in 2-3 specific PM methodologies: Waterfall, Agile, Hybrid, PRINCE2, or Lean
  • Create a comprehensive PM resume highlighting transferable skills and completed projects
  • Update LinkedIn profile with PM focus; publish 2-3 articles on PM topics to demonstrate thought leadership
  • Apply for Project Manager, Associate PM, or Project Coordinator roles (internal and external)
  • Prepare for PM interviews: study behavioral questions, case studies, and situational scenarios
  • Build relationships with hiring managers and recruiters specializing in PM roles

Key Milestones

Earn PMP® certification
Deliver medium-complexity project successfully
Secure Project Manager or equivalent role
Receive internal recognition or promotion to PM track

Roadmap: Technical Support Engineer → Program Manager

Overview: Program Managers operate at a higher strategic level, overseeing multiple interconnected projects (programs) that collectively deliver business objectives. This role requires strong strategic thinking, executive communication, organizational change management, and the ability to align technical initiatives with business strategy. The transition leverages your systems thinking and cross-team collaboration experience while building enterprise-level leadership capabilities.

Aspect Technical Support Engineer Program Manager
Primary Focus Resolving individual customer issues Delivering strategic business outcomes through multiple projects
Scope Individual tickets and escalations Multiple interdependent projects and initiatives
Time Horizon Immediate to short-term Long-term (quarters to years)
Key Stakeholders Customers, engineering teams C-suite executives, business unit leaders, external partners
Success Metrics Ticket resolution, SLA compliance Business value delivery, strategic alignment, ROI, organizational transformation
Leadership Scope Influence within support team Lead multiple project managers and cross-functional teams

Phase 1: Foundation & Project Management Mastery (Months 1-9)

Duration: 9 months | Effort: 15-20 hours/week

Goal: Build strong PM foundation as prerequisite to program management

Critical Context: Program management is typically not an entry-level transition from support engineering. You must first establish credibility as a Project Manager before advancing to Program Manager. This phase focuses on accelerated PM skill development.

Core Competencies to Build

Project Management Fundamentals

All PM core skills: planning, execution, risk management, stakeholder management

Business Strategy

Strategic planning, business case development, value stream mapping, OKRs

Financial Management

Business finance, ROI analysis, portfolio budgeting, cost-benefit analysis

Systems Thinking

Understanding interdependencies, holistic problem-solving, organizational dynamics

Action Checklist

  • Complete comprehensive PM certification: PMP®, PRINCE2 Practitioner, or Google PM Certificate
  • Read "The Standard for Program Management" by PMI - understand program vs. project distinction
  • Lead 3-4 projects successfully as Project Manager or Project Lead
  • Complete business strategy course (MBA-level or equivalent): Porter's Five Forces, SWOT, competitive analysis
  • Take financial management for non-financial managers course (Coursera, edX, or corporate training)
  • Shadow or interview 2-3 Program Managers; understand their daily responsibilities and strategic focus
  • Volunteer to coordinate multiple related projects; practice managing dependencies
  • Build expertise in strategic planning frameworks: OKRs, Balanced Scorecard, Strategy Maps

Recommended Resources

  • The Standard for Program Management - PMI
  • Introduction to Corporate Finance - Wharton/Coursera
  • Business Strategy Courses - edX
  • Technical Program Management Resources - TPM Academy
  • Strategy Articles - Harvard Business Review

Phase 2: Program Management Preparation (Months 10-18)

Duration: 9 months | Effort: 20+ hours/week

Goal: Develop program-level thinking and leadership capabilities

Advanced Program Management Skills

Portfolio Management

Portfolio prioritization, resource optimization, strategic alignment, value maximization

Organizational Change

Change management, stakeholder influence, cultural transformation, adoption strategies

Executive Communication

Board-level presentations, executive summaries, strategic storytelling, influence without authority

Cross-Functional Leadership

Leading without direct reports, matrix management, political navigation, coalition building

Action Checklist

  • Earn Program Management Professional (PgMP®) certification from PMI
  • Secure role as Senior Project Manager or Associate Program Manager
  • Lead a small program: 3+ interdependent projects with shared strategic objective
  • Complete change management certification: Prosci ADKAR, Certified Change Management Professional
  • Develop executive presentation skills: Toastmasters, executive communication courses, executive coaching
  • Create program governance framework: steering committees, RACI matrices, escalation paths
  • Master program management tools: portfolio dashboards, program roadmaps, benefits realization tracking
  • Build business case for a hypothetical program: financial modeling, ROI projection, risk assessment
  • Network with Program Managers in your industry; join TPM (Technical Program Manager) communities

Key Milestones

Earn PgMP® or equivalent program management certification
Successfully coordinate 3+ projects as unified program
Present strategic initiative to executive leadership
Demonstrate measurable business value delivery

Recommended Resources

Strategic Leadership Skills

People Leadership

  • Build comprehensive program portfolio documenting outcomes, business value, lessons learned
  • Update resume and LinkedIn to reflect program management focus and achievements
  • Apply for Program Manager roles internally and externally
  • Prepare for program management interviews: strategic case studies, leadership scenarios, stakeholder management
  • Consider MBA or Executive Master's in Business/Technology Management for strategic credibility

Key Milestones

Lead enterprise-level program successfully
Secure Program Manager or Technical Program Manager role
Deliver measurable business transformation or strategic initiative
Establish reputation as strategic leader and change agent

Roadmap: Technical Support Engineer → Technical Support Manager

Overview: Technical Support Managers lead teams of support specialists, drive operational excellence, develop support strategy, and serve as the bridge between technical operations and business objectives. This is often the most natural transition from TSE, leveraging your deep domain expertise, customer empathy, and understanding of support workflows while building people leadership and operational management capabilities.

Aspect Technical Support Engineer Technical Support Manager
Primary Focus Resolving customer technical issues Leading team performance, operational excellence, strategy
Daily Activities Troubleshooting, customer communication, technical escalations Team coaching, process improvement, metrics analysis, stakeholder management
Success Metrics Personal ticket resolution, SLA compliance, customer satisfaction Team performance, CSAT/NPS, first-contact resolution, operational efficiency
Responsibility Scope Individual contributor Team of 5-15+ support engineers, budget, tools, processes
Decision Authority Technical resolution within scope Hiring, performance management, escalation policies, resource allocation
Time Allocation 90% technical work, 10% administrative 20% technical, 80% leadership/management/strategy

Phase 1: Leadership Foundation (Months 1-4)

Duration: 4 months | Effort: 10-15 hours/week

Goal: Build foundational people leadership and management skills

Pro Tip: Technical Support Manager is the most direct path from TSE. You already possess the technical expertise and customer understanding; focus on developing people leadership, coaching, and operational management skills.

Core Competencies to Build

People Leadership

Team motivation, coaching, feedback delivery, conflict resolution, delegation

Performance Management

Goal setting, performance reviews, development plans, accountability frameworks

Operational Excellence

Process optimization, metrics analysis, SLA management, workflow design

Communication

Team meetings, one-on-ones, executive reporting, cross-functional collaboration

Action Checklist

  • Complete "From Technical Expert to People Leader" or equivalent management fundamentals course
  • Read "The Manager's Path" by Camille Fournier or "First, Break All the Rules" by Marcus Buckingham
  • Volunteer for team lead or senior engineer role; mentor 2-3 junior support engineers
  • Shadow your current manager: observe one-on-ones, team meetings, performance reviews, escalations
  • Complete course on giving and receiving feedback (Crucial Conversations, Radical Candor framework)
  • Develop expertise in support metrics: analyze CSAT, first-contact resolution, average handle time, ticket trends
  • Lead one process improvement initiative: knowledge base update, workflow optimization, tool enhancement
  • Attend management and leadership webinars/workshops focused on first-time managers

Recommended Resources

  • Become a Manager Learning Path - LinkedIn Learning
  • The Manager's Path by Camille Fournier
  • Radical Candor - Leadership Framework
  • Managing People and Teams - Coursera
  • SupportOps - Technical Support Operations Resources

Phase 2: Practical Leadership Experience (Months 5-9)

Duration: 5 months | Effort: 15-20 hours/week

Goal: Gain hands-on team leadership experience and operational management

Advanced Management Skills

Hiring & Onboarding

Recruitment, interviewing, offer negotiation, new hire onboarding, ramp planning

Strategic Planning

Support strategy, capacity planning, service improvement, roadmap development

Budget Management

Budget planning, cost control, resource allocation, tool/vendor management

Cross-Functional Leadership

Collaboration with engineering, product, sales, success teams; escalation management

Action Checklist

  • Secure Team Lead, Senior Support Engineer, or Support Specialist III role with leadership responsibilities
  • Lead a team of 3-5 support engineers (formally or informally); conduct regular one-on-ones
  • Participate in hiring: screen candidates, conduct technical interviews, provide hiring recommendations
  • Develop and deliver technical training for support team: new product features, troubleshooting techniques
  • Own support metrics dashboard: track KPIs, identify trends, present insights to management
  • Lead 2-3 operational improvement projects: reduce handle time, improve CSAT, enhance knowledge base
  • Master support tools: ticketing systems (Zendesk, ServiceNow, Freshdesk), CRM, analytics platforms
  • Complete ITIL Foundation certification (IT Service Management framework)
  • Build relationships with Product and Engineering: advocate for customer needs, escalate systemic issues

Key Milestones

Lead team of 3-5 engineers successfully
Deliver measurable operational improvement
Earn ITIL Foundation or equivalent certification
Receive positive feedback from team and management
